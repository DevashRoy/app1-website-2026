What is Energy Inequality?

Energy inequality occurs when certain groups of people have less access to affordable, clean, and reliable energy sources. Low-income communities and communities of color are often affected the most.

675M

Around 675 million people worldwide still lack access to electricity.

3.8B

Nearly half the global population lacks access to clean cooking fuels.

Higher Bills

Low-income households often spend a larger percentage of income on energy.

Social & Environmental Effects

Health Problems

Pollution from fossil fuels increases asthma, respiratory disease, and other health risks in vulnerable communities.

Economic Challenges

High energy costs make it harder for families to afford necessities like food, housing, and healthcare.

Climate Risks

Marginalized communities are more vulnerable to heat waves, flooding, and power outages caused by climate change.

Global Examples

Energy inequality affects communities differently around the world. Many rural and low-income populations struggle to access renewable energy.

Click to Learn More About Global Examples

India

Rural communities in India often face unreliable electricity access. Community solar programs are helping villages gain cleaner and more dependable energy while reducing fossil fuel use.

Source: World Bank Energy Program

Sub-Saharan Africa

Millions of people in Sub-Saharan Africa still live without consistent electricity. Renewable microgrids and solar systems provide affordable energy access to remote communities.

Source: International Energy Agency (IEA)

United States

In the United States, low-income neighborhoods and communities of color often face higher electricity costs and fewer renewable energy opportunities. Community solar projects are helping make clean energy more accessible.

Source: U.S. Department of Energy

Possible Solutions

Community Solar

Shared solar projects allow multiple households to benefit from renewable energy even if they cannot install panels themselves.

Government Programs

Subsidies and energy assistance programs can help make renewable energy more affordable.

Energy Democracy

Giving communities more control over energy production creates fairer and more sustainable systems.
